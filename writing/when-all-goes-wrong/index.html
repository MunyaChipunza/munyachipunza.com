Faith

When All Seems to Be Going Wrong

Some seasons are defined by collapse. Plans unravel, timing disappoints, and the life you tried to build becomes harder to recognize.

Munya Chipunza October 2025 4 min read

There are stretches of life when nothing dramatic happens all at once, yet everything still feels wrong. Doors close. Timelines shift. Money tightens. Relationships strain. You keep moving, but the overall picture feels like it is losing shape.

Those seasons can be especially disorienting because they do not always provide a clear villain. There is no single event to point at, no neat explanation to offer when someone asks what is going on. Things are simply not working the way you hoped they would.

Faith can feel fragile in moments like that. Not because you stop believing in God, but because your imagination gets tired. It becomes difficult to picture a future that still makes sense. You pray, but the prayers sound thinner than they used to. You keep showing up, but the confidence is gone.

Sometimes faith is not fire. Sometimes it is the refusal to walk away while the room is still dark.

I think we often expect faith to feel stronger than it actually does in real life. We imagine certainty, boldness, and clarity. But many hard seasons do not offer those things. They offer the next step and little else. Enough light for today, not enough light for the entire year.

That can feel frustrating if you like control, timelines, and coherent plans. But it may also be where trust becomes real. Not as a slogan, but as a daily practice: do the next right thing, tell the truth, keep your word, rest when you can, ask for help, pray again, and refuse to let bitterness become your strategy.

Going wrong does not always mean being abandoned. Delay is not proof of divine absence. Confusion is not the same as failure. There are seasons when the work of faith is simply to stay soft while life is hard.

If everything around you feels less secure than it used to, do not rush to force a grand meaning out of it. Sometimes steadiness comes first, understanding later. Sometimes all you are being asked for today is the next honest act of obedience.

Hold the day that is in front of you. Let tomorrow arrive when it is ready. That, too, can be faith.
