Sometimes It Is Ok to Be Not Ok

There are seasons when pretending to be fine becomes more damaging than the struggle you are trying to hide.

Munya Chipunza October 2025 3 min read

Sometimes life does not let up. You carry one load, then another arrives before your hands have even adjusted to the first one. The body keeps moving, but the soul starts dragging.

In those seasons, people often tell you to stay strong. The problem is that strength can become a costume. You wear it because everyone around you feels safer when you do. They may not mean harm, but the message lands all the same: hold yourself together for us.

Eventually you become fluent in saying, "I am fine," when your inner life is anything but fine. You answer quickly. You smile on cue. You keep appointments. You meet deadlines. You remain functional enough that no one suspects how expensive your composure has become.

Functioning is not the same as flourishing. Sometimes it is just survival with better posture.

There is wisdom in admitting when the soul is tired. Not dramatic collapse for its own sake, but honest recognition. I am not coping well right now. I need rest. I need prayer. I need help. I need space to tell the truth without also having to reassure everyone else.

Being not ok is not moral failure. It is not spiritual disqualification. It is not evidence that your faith has vanished. Sometimes it is simply evidence that you are a finite person living inside finite limits while carrying real grief, pressure, disappointment, or fear.

Jesus never asked exhausted people to pretend they were not exhausted. He invited the weary to come. Not to perform wellness. To come. That matters.

If today is one of those days when your answer to "How are you?" would be more honest if it were quieter and less polished, do not despise that truth. It may be the most faithful thing you have said all week.

You do not need to glamorize your pain. But you also do not need to hide it from yourself in order to remain respectable.
