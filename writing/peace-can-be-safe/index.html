Peace Can Be Safe, Too

Sometimes calm feels suspicious because your body has spent too long preparing for the next interruption, the next loss, the next demand.

Munya Chipunza October 2025 3 min read

There are seasons when peace arrives and instead of receiving it, you brace against it. The room gets quiet and your mind immediately starts scanning for what is about to go wrong.

It is not because you dislike peace. It is because your body has been taught another language. Pressure, urgency, disappointment, over-responsibility, and disruption have trained your nervous system to feel most familiar when something is wrong.

So when a gentle day appears, the first instinct is not gratitude. It is suspicion. You wait for the phone call, the conflict, the bad news, the bill, the delay, the emotional shift, the hidden cost. Calm feels temporary. Safety feels borrowed.

Sometimes peace feels unsafe only because chaos has become the environment you know best.

This is one of the quieter consequences of hard seasons. Even after circumstances improve, the body can keep living as though danger remains. The calendar may have changed, but the inner alarm has not yet learned that it can stand down.

That is why peace sometimes requires practice. Not performance. Practice. Letting a quiet evening be quiet. Letting a kind moment remain kind without immediately questioning it. Letting your shoulders soften without demanding a reason first.

Prayer can help here, but not always in the dramatic way people imagine. Sometimes prayer is just staying present long enough to notice that nothing terrible happened in the last ten minutes. Sometimes it is gratitude without exaggeration. Sometimes it is whispering, Lord, teach my body what my mind already knows.

You do not become weak by learning how to rest. You do not become naive by receiving a calm season with open hands. Peace is not a trap. It is not laziness. It is not irresponsibility. It is also part of what we are meant to inhabit.

If calm feels unfamiliar right now, do not condemn yourself for that. Just keep teaching your heart that quiet does not always mean something bad is hiding nearby.
